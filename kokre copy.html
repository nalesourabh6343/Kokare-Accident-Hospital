First Aid Instructions

Emergency first aid guidance for fractures, dislocations, and bone injuries. Know what to do before the ambulance arrives.

Fracture Care Immobilization RICE Method
View First Aid Guide
First Aid for Bone Injuries
For Suspected Fractures
  • Do NOT move the injured limb
  • Apply ice wrapped in cloth (not directly)
  • Immobilize with splint if available
  • Keep the person calm and still
RICE Method
  • Rest - Stop using the injured area
  • Ice - Apply cold pack for 20 min
  • Compression - Wrap with bandage
  • Elevation - Raise above heart level
Important: Call emergency services immediately for severe injuries, open fractures, or if the person is unconscious.

Life Saving Tips

The Golden Hour Protocol

The first 60 minutes after an accident are critical. Following these steps can prevent further injury and save lives.

01
Dial for Help

Immediately call our 24/7 Emergency Line at 1800-123-4567. Give the exact location and nature of the accident.

02
Immobilize

Avoid moving the victim unless there is a fire or explosion risk. If a limb is fractured, use a splint or rolled newspapers to keep it still.

03
Control Bleeding

Apply firm, steady pressure to any bleeding wounds using a clean cloth or bandage. Keep the wound elevated if possible.

04
Reassure

Keep the victim calm and warm. Do not give them anything to eat or drink, as it might interfere with potential emergency surgery.
